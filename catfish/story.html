Have you ever been on the Oskil River, which flows through our Kharkiv Oblast to the Siverskyi Donets? You haven't? Then you should visit!

The floodplain of the Oskil River, where it splits into several narrow branches at this point, is overgrown with thick reeds, kuga, bay willows, and thick, green juicy grass.

Between the reeds and the willow tree, there are lots and lots of small ponds covered with thick green rushes and water lilies with yellow-hot flowers on long green whips! And how many water lilies there are! Those small lakes are connected to each other by narrow channels, so narrow that it is barely possible to push through them on a small dugout canoe from one small lake to another.

The magical river Oskil... And how many wild ducks there are! If you happen to be lucky enough to visit those places in the spring or summer, get on a dugout boat and sail quietly, quietly, so that your oar neither splashes nor bubbles...

Magical places on the river in Oskil...

Quietly, quietly Oskil carries water...

Just across the state farm, the river in Oskil is broad; the water is very clean. A little to the left is a deep river hole, a whole sheer pit with calm water above. - There is such a depth that you can't even reach the bottom! No one has ever reached the bottom there! More than that! It is so deep there that our bell tower will dive! Such depth there! This is what Grandfather Panko told us.

- God forbid you swim there! It draws in, deeply draws in! It will spin you, whirl you, plop—and there is no man!

— But it seems to be quiet on the surface, but it whirls under water. And twists and turns! But that's still nothing! Catfishes live there! There lived such a catfish; save, Lord, and have mercy!
